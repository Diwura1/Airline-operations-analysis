# Airline-operations-analysis
Data-driven airline operations dashboard uncovering insights on pricing, demand patterns, and route performance.

# About the Project
This project showcases an interactive airline operations dashboard built using Microsoft Power BI.
The goal was simple: take a raw aviation dataset and turn it into clear, actionable insights that can support smarter business decisions in airline operations.
It focuses on answering key questions around revenue, pricing, route efficiency, and customer behavior through a clean and intuitive dashboard experience.

# Business Problem
Airline stakeholders need visibility into:
Revenue performance
Route efficiency
Pricing trends
Customer booking behavior
Peak travel periods
Competitive positioning across airlines
The dataset contained useful variables (flight duration, ticket prices, stops, class types, airlines, departure times) but lacked structure and business context.

# My Approach
Cleaned and structured the dataset for analysis.
Then created key DAX measures such as;
Total Revenue
Average Ticket Price
Average Flight Duration

# Key Metrics
 Total Flights: 300K
 Total Revenue: ₹6 Billion
 Average Ticket Price: ₹21K
 Average Flight Duration: 12 hours

 # Key Insights & Business Impact
 ### Revenue & Market Positioning
Strong revenue performance across airlines.
Clear pricing tier such as;
Premium airlines (~₹30K)
Budget airlines (~₹4–6K)
Insight: The market follows a tiered pricing strategy, not uniform pricing.

 ### Booking Behavior & Pricing Strategy
Prices are highest for early bookings (~₹30K).
Drop closer to departure (~₹18–20K).
Opportunity: Implement better demand forecasting models to maximize revenue per seat.

 ### Route Performance
Longest route: Kolkata → Chennai (~14.8 hrs)
Shortest route: Bangalore → Delhi (~9.8 hrs)
Insight:
 Long-haul routes likely generate higher revenue but may also have higher operational costs.
  Further cost analysis can reveal true profitability.

### Customer Class Preferences
Economy: 68.85%
Business: 31.15%
Insight:
 Business class likely contributes a larger share of revenue despite lower volume.
Recommendation:
Introduce premium economy options
Improve upselling strategies
Target frequent business travelers

### Flight Stops Analysis
One-stop flights dominate (~83.6%)
Insight:
 Airline networks are optimized for hub-based routing.
Opportunity:
Promote non-stop premium routes
Optimize layover experiences

### High-Traffic Cities
Top routes include:
Mumbai
Delhi
Bangalore
Kolkata
Hyderabad
Chennai
Insight:
 Demand is heavily concentrated in metro cities, driven by business and corporate travel.

### Competitive Pricing by Airline
Premium: Vistara (~₹30K)
Mid-tier: Air India (~₹23K)
Budget: Indigo, AirAsia, SpiceJet (~₹4–6K)
Insight:
 A clearly segmented market where:
Premium airlines compete on experience
Budget airlines compete on price

### Peak Travel Times
Busiest: Morning & Early Morning
Lowest: Late Night
Insight:
High demand aligns with business travel schedules
Late-night slots may be underutilized opportunities
